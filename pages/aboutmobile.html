About Me

I have never minded the idea of working in this world. I understand that in order to achieve a goal, you must put in the work. So when it came to choosing what field of work to pursue, I knew it had to be something creative.

The first story I wrote was during my sixth-grade Language Arts class. On Fridays, we had a free-writing day. Throughout the year, I wrote a sci-fi adventure that included the characters from the video game Smash Bros. Melee. I never finished it, but I do remember my teacher and friends thinking it was cool. Though my desire to be a writer didn’t bloom until college, it was the start of my current journey.

The beauty of college for a mind like mine is the access to learn even more about topics you’ve always been interested in. Nothing excited me more than having the opportunity to take a class on African American Literature. It was in this class that I met the professor who would inspire me to change from a major in Business to English and pursue creative writing.

These days, I’m a writer focused on producing compelling and persuasive long- and short-form copy while developing my voice in creative writing. I am interested in creating works that accentuate culture, art, and identity and display vivid storytelling for all.

Writing Goals for 2026:

  • Write 200-400 words a day.
  • Develop as a creative writer and copywriter.
  • Publish at least 3 short stories and one novella.
  • Publish at least 2 articles a month on my Substack.
  • Join a club for creative writers.
  • Complete my first novel.
